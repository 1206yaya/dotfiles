Whenever I Forget Someone’s Name, I Always Use This Brilliant Hack Avoid potentially embarrassing situations at functions or while networking by remembering this brilliant method. Peter Mukherjee ILLUMINATION Peter Mukherjee · Follow Published in ILLUMINATION · 6 min read · May 30, 2024 9.95K 187 an image of a function in a grand hotel with guests mingling and talking with each other image created in Midjourney Many years ago, I was at a business function at the Ritz Hotel in London. It was a big event, and lots of very important people were there. I was a marketing consultant at the time, hoping to hook up with some interesting potential clients. After an excellently choreographed presentation, all guests were invited to mingle over drinks and canapés for a couple of hours. I was on my own that day and was happy to circulate and do a little networking. After about fifteen minutes of chatting with a charming couple, it was naturally time to move on. We exchanged the usual politenesses: ‘It was really nice to meet you’… ‘and you too’… and we then parted company. While I was chatting with the couple, I was aware of a guy who had been lingering nearby. As soon as I turned around, he looked at me with a big smile and said, ‘Peter, how are you? I thought it was you!’ I recognised his face immediately. He was someone who had been very helpful to me professionally a few years back when I was acting as the spokesperson for a trade association. He was a respected political lobbyist and well-known, too. But the big problem at that moment was, although I had met him a couple of times before, I just couldn’t remember his name! ‘Hello there!’ I said politely in a cheerful manner that (hopefully) didn’t reveal that I was struggling to recall his name. We shook hands like long-lost buddies. ‘I’m really good, thank you. How are you?’ I continued. I was sure it would come back to me over the course of our conversation. But of course, it never did. We carried on talking happily for about ten minutes. Then, a most unfortunate scenario would unfold that I will never forget. The event organiser, a very senior lady who was also one of the keynote speakers, came over to us. I had met her on a couple of occasions before. She acknowledged me: ‘Hello Peter! Are you OK?’ ‘All good, thank you, Elanor. How are you?’ I replied. During our hellos, she had already fixed her gaze firmly on ‘the gentleman’ I was talking to. She was definitely more interested in him than in me! They looked at each other in silence, smiling… Waiting for an introduction from me. But all I could do was watch (I was still digging into every recess of my mind, desperately trying to recall his bloody name!). He quickly glanced at me and then looked back at her. She also glanced at me (with a bemused expression) and then looked back at him. But sadly, it just wouldn’t come back to me… ‘Elanor Beatty!’ she finally burst. ‘Patrick Collins. Very lovely to meet you!’ (not quite their real names…) ‘I saw you in the front row earlier and was very much hoping for an opportunity to meet you’, she said while glancing at me again with a definite hint of disapproval. They carried on talking, and it soon developed into a very friendly conversation. But for me, of course, it was an embarrassing moment. After watching and listening to them engage for a little while and getting on really well, I thought it would be best to let them carry on. ‘I’ll leave you both to it,’ I said sheepishly. They both completely ignored me and carried on talking. I quietly shrank away, looking for the nearest deep hole I could jump into. It was horrible. I felt completely inept. I’d really let my side down. But what the hell else can you do when you completely forget someone’s name? A few years later, I was around for dinner at my parents. In conversation, the subject of ‘awkward situations’ was being entertained, and I took the opportunity to recount my tale of that awful moment at the Ritz.
