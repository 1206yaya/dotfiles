To my surprise, my mother then said to me, ‘Didn’t I ever tell you about your grandad’s brilliant technique that he always used in those situations?’ ‘No, Mum, I don’t believe you did!’ I replied sternly (mockingly). My grandfather had been a leading academic in his day. He would have attended loads of functions over the course of his career. His memory was never that brilliant (I obviously inherited that flaw) and he would obviously have had an issue remembering people’s names, too. My mother then explained his special technique to me and precisely how I should apply it, next time… It was brilliant advice. I have used it on several occasions over many years since, in those awkward situations, and it has always worked like a dream… So, here is how it goes… Prelude: You are at an event, talking to someone, and you simply can’t remember their name. You can’t get away with ‘blagging it’ either, as I had tried to do at the Ritz, in case the same thing that happened to me happens to you. In other words, you have to find out their name… There are three possible scenarios, each with a slightly different variation of this technique, to apply. The first-name scenario You say to them (looking straight into their eyes and always with a big smile): ‘I’m really sorry, but I can’t remember your name?’ ‘John’ (they say, perhaps feeling hurt, annoyed or just a bit disappointed…) ‘I know it's John! It’s your second name I can’t quite recall…’ ‘Smith’ ‘Smith!! Yes of course. I’m sorry John, just had a complete mental block for a moment!’ [ So, the logic here is that this is much less worse than appearing not to know their whole name at all. It’s perfectly plausible to have forgotten their second name and quite forgivable, too ] The second-name scenario ‘I’m really sorry, but I can’t remember your name?’ ‘Smith’ ‘I know it's Smith! It’s your first name I can’t quite recall…’ ‘John’ ‘John!! Of course. Sorry John, just had a complete mental block for a moment!’ [ Just about plausible. One doesn’t often forget someone’s first name while remembering their second (unless you are a school teacher), but definitely not as bad as forgetting their whole name! One can get away with it… ] The whole-name scenario ‘I’m really sorry, but I can’t remember your name.’ ‘John Smith’ ‘I know it’s John!! It was just your second name I couldn’t quite recall. I’m sorry, just had a complete mental block for a moment!’ [ Again, perfectly plausible — it sounds like you just wanted to make sure you recalled their second name correctly ] This is a very simple technique, and it works. It’s much better than the embarrassment I had to endure, and it definitely avoids the stress. Is it being less than honest? To me, as soon as someone says their name ‘John’, you then know it’s ‘John’. So, to then say, ‘I know it’s John! It’s your second name I can’t quite recall…’ is quite reasonable… Most of the time, people think it’s funny. And it soon gets forgotten as you engage further in conversation. Thankfully, I never had to meet with either ‘Elanor’ or ‘Patrick’ again. Should you ever find yourself in the same (potentially) embarrassing situation as me, I hope this can be a lifesaver for you, too.
